## Overview

This block diagram represents the motor subsystem controlled by a PIC18F47K42 microcontroller. The system is powered by a 12 V 7 Ah battery, which supplies energy to two voltage regulation stages that provide stable power to the control electronics and motors. A 3.3 V switching regulator (AP63203WU-7) generates the logic-level supply used by the microcontroller and other digital circuitry, ensuring safe and consistent operation of the control system.

The PIC18F47K42 serves as the central controller and communicates with the system through several digital and PWM-capable I/O pins. The device is programmed through a Microchip SNAP programmer using the ICSP interface. Two PWM signals generated by the microcontroller are sent to the motor driver to regulate motor speed, while additional digital lines control the direction of motor rotation.

Two override/system check buttons are included to support manual testing and diagnostic procedures. These inputs provide digital signals that allow the system to verify motor driver functionality and motor behavior independently of the firmware.

The motor driver receives PWM and control signals from the microcontroller and converts them into higher-current outputs capable of driving the motors. A 6 V voltage regulator (L7806ABD2T) supplies the appropriate voltage for the motors, ensuring they operate within their rated electrical limits.

Overall, this subsystem provides regulated power distribution, PWM-based motor speed control, and manual override capability, enabling reliable motor operation and facilitating system testing during development.
